4. **Create a Dedicated “Projects / Portfolio” Section**  
   - List 2‑3 relevant projects (personal, academic, or open‑source) with:  
     - Project name, brief description, tech stack, link to repo or demo, and measurable impact.  
   - This adds keyword density (e.g., Docker, CI/CD, AWS Lambda) and showcases hands‑on work.

5. **List Certifications & Training**  
   - Include any relevant credentials (e.g., “AWS Certified Developer – Associate (2023)”, “Python Institute – PCAP‑Python Certified Associate Programmer”).  

6. **Expand the Skills Section**  
   - Add missing but common backend keywords: *Django/Flask, PostgreSQL, Redis, Docker, Kubernetes, CI/CD (Jenkins/GitHub Actions), Test‑Driven Development, Agile/Scrum*.  
   - Use a simple bullet or pipe‑separated format for ATS readability.

7. **Provide Full Education Details**  
   - Include institution name, graduation year, GPA (if ≥ 3.5), relevant coursework, honors, or thesis topic.

8. **Add Optional but Helpful Sections**  
   - *Awards & Publications* – any recognitions, conference talks, blog posts.  
   - *Professional Affiliations* – e.g., member of ACM, local Python user group.  
   - *Languages* – spoken languages or additional programming languages.

9. **Apply ATS‑Friendly Formatting**  
   - Use **standard headings** (Education, Experience, Skills, Projects, Certifications).  
   - Keep **one‑column layout**, avoid tables/graphics, and use simple bullet points.  
   - Ensure **consistent bullet style** (e.g., “•” or “-”) and **uniform spacing**.  
   - Save the final document as a **plain .docx** or **PDF (text‑based, not scanned)** to preserve parseability.

10. **Keyword Optimization**  
    - Review the target job description and **mirror the exact phrasing** of required skills/technologies in the resume (e.g., “microservices”, “CI/CD pipelines”, “Agile”).  
    - Place the most important keywords in the **top third** of the document (summary, skills, experience bullets) to increase match probability.
