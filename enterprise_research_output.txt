## 1. Executive Summary  

Agentic AI represents the next evolution of enterprise automation: autonomous, reasoning‑capable agents that can **plan, orchestrate, and execute multi‑step workflows across disparate business systems** while operating under explicit governance and human oversight.  

- **Strategic relevance:** 71 % of surveyed organizations expect AI agents to increase automation levels; 64 % anticipate direct improvements in customer‑service satisfaction.  
- **Maturity:** Proven platforms (e.g., Moveworks Reasoning Engine, Capgemini’s Agentic AI suite) already deliver production‑grade capabilities, reducing early‑adopter risk.  
- **Business impact:** Early adopters can achieve **20‑30 % reductions in manual processing time**, **15‑25 % cost savings**, **5‑10‑point lifts in NPS**, and **10‑15 % productivity gains**—all while maintaining compliance through built‑in audit trails and policy enforcement.  

Given the high strategic importance, strong market momentum, and emerging best‑practice governance frameworks, **Enterprise Agentic AI merits a “high‑priority” investment** in the next 12‑24 months.

## 3. Risks  

| Risk Category | Description | Potential Impact | Mitigation |
|---------------|-------------|------------------|------------|
| **Governance & Compliance** | Autonomous decisions may inadvertently breach policy if guardrails are insufficient. | Regulatory fines, reputational damage. | Deploy platforms with **policy‑as‑code**, continuous monitoring, and human‑in‑the‑loop checkpoints. |
| **Integration Complexity** | Connecting agents to legacy systems (ERP, mainframes) can be technically demanding. | Project delays, cost overruns. | Adopt **API‑first** architectures, use pre‑built connectors, and phase integration with low‑risk pilot processes. |
| **Model Drift & Accuracy** | LLM reasoning quality may degrade over time as data evolves. | Incorrect actions, loss of trust. | Implement **continuous model evaluation**, retraining pipelines, and fallback rule‑based processes. |
| **Security Surface Expansion** | More autonomous interactions increase exposure to attacks (e.g., prompt injection). | Data breach, service disruption. | Enforce **zero‑trust** network segmentation, input sanitization, and strict authentication for agent APIs. |
| **Change‑Management Resistance** | Employees may fear job displacement or loss of control. | Low adoption, cultural friction. | Launch **upskilling programs**, define clear **human‑agent collaboration** roles, and communicate tangible productivity benefits. |
| **Vendor Lock‑In** | Proprietary agent platforms may limit future flexibility. | Higher switching costs. | Favor **open‑standard** APIs and modular architectures; negotiate exit clauses. |

## 5. Strategic Recommendations  

| Recommendation | Rationale | Timeline | Owner(s) |
|----------------|-----------|----------|----------|
| **1. Initiate a Pilot Program** – Deploy an agentic platform (e.g., Moveworks Reasoning Engine) on a high‑impact, low‑risk process such as IT service‑desk ticket triage. | Provides concrete ROI data, validates integration and governance frameworks, and builds internal expertise. | Q1‑Q2 2026 | Head of Digital Transformation + IT Service Management |
| **2. Build a Governance Framework** – Define policy‑as‑code, audit‑log standards, and human‑in‑the‑loop thresholds. | Ensures compliance, mitigates security risks, and establishes trust among stakeholders. | Parallel with pilot (Month 1‑3) | Chief Risk Officer + Legal |
| **3. Create an Integration Architecture Blueprint** – Map all target systems (ERP, CRM, HRIS) and select pre‑built connectors or develop custom APIs. | Reduces integration complexity and future‑proofs the solution. | Months 2‑4 | Enterprise Architecture Team |
| **4. Upskill the Workforce** – Launch a “Agentic AI Academy” covering prompt engineering, agent oversight, and change‑management best practices. | Drives adoption, alleviates employee concerns, and cultivates internal talent. | Ongoing; initial cohort by Q2 2026 | HR Learning & Development |
| **5. Establish a Center of Excellence (CoE)** – Centralize expertise on agentic AI, maintain a repository of reusable agent templates, and monitor market developments. | Ensures continuous innovation, shares learnings across business units, and avoids siloed implementations. | Launch by Q3 2026 | Chief Innovation Officer |
| **6. Define Success Metrics & ROI Model** – Quantify targets for cost reduction, CX improvement, and productivity gains; embed in annual budgeting. | Provides measurable accountability and justifies further investment. | Q2 2026 (baseline) | Finance & Strategy Office |
| **7. Evaluate Vendor Ecosystem & Open Standards** – Assess multiple agentic platforms for interoperability, pricing, and lock‑in risk; prefer solutions with open APIs. | Mitigates vendor dependence and enables future flexibility. | Ongoing; review annually | Procurement & IT |
